Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

Early Life

Gandhi was born on the 2nd of October, 1869, in the city of Porbandar, in present-day Gujarat, and was named Mohandas Gandhi. He came from a Hindu family and his father served as the dewan, or chief minister, of several small princely states. As a child, Gandhi was shy and withdrawn. He was also deeply religious, spending hours reading religious texts such as the Bhagavad Gita.In 1883, at the age of thirteen, Gandhi married 14-year-old Kasturba Makhanji, a young girl who had been selected for him by his parents.

Contributions

Assuming leadership of the Indian National Congress in 1921, Gandhi led nationwide campaigns for easing poverty, expanding women's rights, building religious and ethnic amity, ending untouchability, and, above all,achieving swaraj or self-rule.The most important point in Gandhi's scheme of education is its emphasis on relating school education to the needs of the society. He wanted to achieve this objective through a system of Learning while earning. He gave an important place to the learning of craft.

Legacy

In some sense, Gandhi's greatest achievement lay in his legacy; for his ideals, and the example he provided in living them out, inspired, and continue to inspire, people of all nations to take up the peaceful struggle for freedom from oppression. Mahatma Gandhi's contribution to the Indian freedom struggle was immense. His philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience, his tactics of non-cooperation and civil disobedience, and his leadership united the Indian masses and forced the British to confront the demands of the Indian people.
